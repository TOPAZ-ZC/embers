real questions

Real questions.
Real answers.

We'd rather tell you what this isn't than oversell what it is.

When does Embers launch?

Coming Summer 2026. Spark ships first (Self-Hosted and Cloud), followed by Ember and Hearth within the first month. Bonfire and Forge land in phase two. Waitlist signups get early access + founders pricing locked at signup — your tier stays at that price forever, even when post-launch prices go up.

Self-Hosted vs Cloud — which one should I pick?

Self-Hosted runs Embers on your own Mac — you get blue-bubble iMessage out of the box and we charge less because you're running the bridge. Cloud is for when you don't want to run anything locally or you need SMS reach on Android recipients — we host the bridge and cover the SMS cost. Features are identical on both. Not sure? Pick "undecided" on the waitlist and we'll walk you through it at launch.

Is this going to pretend to be me? Isn't that weird?

It sends replies in your voice, from your Mac (Self-Hosted) or our bridge (Cloud), from your phone number. That's the point — the thread stays warm. Some people tell the person. Some don't. That's on you. Embers isn't trying to pretend to be you forever — it's trying to keep the thread alive until you show up in it again.

How does "per-thread voice" actually work?

When you add a thread, Embers asks how you text that specific person. Short and dry? Warm and exclamation-heavy? Professional? You describe it in plain English, and you can paste up to 10 of your own past messages for a tighter match. Every reply to that thread uses only that voice.

What is Catch-up?

When you've been heads-down for a week, open Embers' menu bar, tap "catch me up on Dad" — you get a 3-sentence summary of what's happened since you last engaged. What's the news? What's he waiting on? What's the vibe? It keeps YOU in the loop, not just the thread.

Where does my data go?

Self-Hosted: your iMessage history stays on your Mac. Embers reads it read-only. When a reply needs to be drafted, it sends the last handful of messages in that one thread to our proxy, which calls an LLM and returns a reply. Token counts stored, not message content. Privacy-Hard mode (Bonfire+) runs inference locally — nothing leaves at all. Cloud: same draft-time flow, bridge hosted by us, same no-retention policy on content.

Can I use this for work?

Yes — that's what Hearth, Bonfire, and Forge are for. Realtors run their lead pipeline. Recruiters warm candidates. SDRs keep sequences alive. CRM sync on Bonfire (HubSpot, Pipedrive) and Forge (Salesforce, HubSpot). Check local regulations on AI-assisted outreach disclosure — some states and industries have rules, and they're on you to follow.
